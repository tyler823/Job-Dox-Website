Job Status & Phases

5 min readBeginner

Every job in Job-Dox moves through a series of statuses that reflect its current stage — from first contact to final closeout. Changing a job's status keeps your entire team aligned and can trigger powerful automations behind the scenes.

Pro Tip

Changing a job's status can trigger automations — like sending a client update or creating follow-up tasks.

Step-by-Step

1
Open any job from the dashboard

Click a job card from your Active Jobs list or use the search bar to find the job by name or client.

2
Find the Status dropdown

At the top of the job detail view, you'll see the current status badge next to the job name. Click it to open the dropdown.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
3
Select the new status

Each status represents a distinct phase of the project:

  • New Lead — Initial inquiry, not yet started
  • Active — Work is underway
  • Mitigation — Emergency mitigation phase (water extraction, board-up, etc.)
  • Reconstruction — Rebuild and restoration phase
  • Pending Approval — Waiting on client or insurance sign-off
  • Completed — All work finished
  • Closed — Fully closed out and archived
  • On Hold — Job is paused (e.g., waiting on materials or insurance)
4
Review the phase timeline

The timeline at the top of the job shows how the job has progressed through each phase.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
5
Save — automations fire automatically

Once you select a new status, it saves immediately. Any automations tied to that status (client notifications, task creation, team alerts) trigger in the background.

Notes & Activity Log

4 min readBeginner

Daily notes are the backbone of job documentation in Job-Dox. Every note is timestamped, attributed to the user who created it, and fully searchable — giving your team a complete, auditable history of every job.

Important

Notes are permanent records. Once saved, they cannot be deleted — only amended. This protects documentation integrity for insurance claims.

Step-by-Step

1
Open a job and click the Notes tab

From the job detail view, select the Notes tab in the navigation bar to see all existing notes and the activity timeline.

2
Click "+ Add Note"

A text editor appears where you can type your daily update, site observations, or client communication summary.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
3
Add photos or files to the note

Drag and drop images or documents directly onto the note editor, or click the Attach button to browse your files. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, PDF, and DOCX.

4
View the activity log

The activity log shows a chronological timeline of everything that has happened on the job — notes, uploads, status changes, and more.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
5
Search and filter notes

Use the search bar at the top of the Notes tab to find specific entries by keyword. You can also filter by date range or team member to narrow down results.

Creating Tasks

5 min readBeginner

Tasks break a job into actionable items your crew can track and complete. Every task belongs to a job, has an assignee, due date, and status.

Warning

A task without a due date won't appear in Calendar View or trigger deadline reminders.

Step-by-Step

1
Open a job and click the Tasks tab

Navigate to any active job from your dashboard, then select the Tasks tab to see the current task list for that job.

2
Click "+ New Task"

This opens the task creation form where you fill in all details for the new task.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
3
Set priority level

Choose the appropriate priority for the task. Low (gray) is for routine items, Medium (blue) for standard work, High (orange) for time-sensitive items, and Urgent (red) for immediate action required.

4
Add a checklist (optional)

Break a task into smaller sub-tasks by adding checklist items. Each item can be checked off independently, giving you granular progress tracking within a single task.

5
Save the task

Click Create Task to save. The task immediately appears in the job's task list and on the assignee's personal dashboard.

Recurring Tasks

3 min readIntermediate

Recurring tasks auto-create on a schedule — perfect for weekly safety checks, equipment inspections, or daily moisture readings.

Warning

Recurring tasks are created based on the job's start date. If the job is On Hold, recurring generation pauses until it's reactivated.

Step-by-Step

1
Create a task as normal

Start by creating a task the usual way (see Creating Tasks). Fill in the task name, assignee, and priority as you normally would.

2
Toggle "Repeat" on

Enable the Repeat toggle to reveal the recurrence settings panel where you configure the schedule.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
3
Set the frequency

Choose how often the task repeats: Daily, Weekly, Bi-weekly, or Monthly. For weekly and bi-weekly, select which days of the week the task should be created on.

4
Save the recurring task

Click Create Task to save. The system will automatically generate new task instances on schedule. Each instance can be completed independently without affecting the others.

Webhooks

5 min readAdvanced

Webhooks let external systems receive real-time notifications when events happen in Job-Dox — project created, status changed, task completed, document signed, and more. Register an endpoint URL, select the events you care about, and Job-Dox will POST event data to your URL as it happens. Perfect for Zapier, Make, or custom integrations.

Pro tip

Use the "webhook.test" event to verify your endpoint is receiving payloads before subscribing to production events. This avoids silent failures in your integrations.

Step-by-Step: Registering a Webhook

1
Navigate to Settings › Integrations › Webhooks

Open the Webhooks management page to see existing endpoints and their delivery status.

2
Add a new webhook endpoint

Enter your endpoint URL and select which events should trigger deliveries. Choose from project events, task events, document events, and more.

See your live portal at app.job-dox.ai
3
Test your endpoint

Click "Send Test" to fire a webhook.test event to your URL. Check the delivery log for a 200 status code confirming your endpoint received the payload.

4
Monitor delivery health

The delivery log shows every webhook attempt with HTTP status, response time, and timestamp. Failed deliveries (4xx/5xx) are retried automatically with exponential backoff.

Important

Webhook endpoints must respond with a 2xx status within 10 seconds. If your endpoint consistently times out or returns errors, deliveries will be paused after 10 consecutive failures.
