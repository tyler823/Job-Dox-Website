Job Status & Phases

5 min readBeginner

Every job in Job-Dox moves through a series of statuses that reflect its current stage — from first contact to final closeout. Changing a job's status keeps your entire team aligned and can trigger powerful automations behind the scenes.

Pro Tip

Changing a job's status can trigger automations — like sending a client update or creating follow-up tasks.

Step-by-Step

1
Open any job from the dashboard

Click a job card from your Active Jobs list or use the search bar to find the job by name or client.

2
Find the Status dropdown

At the top of the job detail view, you'll see the current status badge next to the job name. Click it to open the dropdown.

3
Select the new status

Each status represents a distinct phase of the project:

  • New Lead — Initial inquiry, not yet started
  • Active — Work is underway
  • Mitigation — Emergency mitigation phase (water extraction, board-up, etc.)
  • Reconstruction — Rebuild and restoration phase
  • Pending Approval — Waiting on client or insurance sign-off
  • Completed — All work finished
  • Closed — Fully closed out and archived
  • On Hold — Job is paused (e.g., waiting on materials or insurance)
4
Review the phase timeline

The timeline at the top of the job shows how the job has progressed through each phase.

3 min readIntermediate

Recurring tasks auto-create on a schedule — perfect for weekly safety checks, equipment inspections, or daily moisture readings.

Warning

Recurring tasks are created based on the job's start date. If the job is On Hold, recurring generation pauses until it's reactivated.

Step-by-Step

1
Create a task as normal

Start by creating a task the usual way (see Creating Tasks). Fill in the task name, assignee, and priority as you normally would.

2
Toggle "Repeat" on

Enable the Repeat toggle to reveal the recurrence settings panel where you configure the schedule.

Set the frequency

4
Save the recurring task

Click Create Task to save. The system will automatically generate new task instances on schedule. Each instance can be completed independently without affecting the others.
