Part 1
Getting Started — Setup Sequence

Complete these nine steps in order before using the platform with your team. Each step builds on the one before it — skipping ahead will cause features to appear incomplete or broken.

🧭
Step 1 — Explore Advanced Tools & Navigation
All Staff · ~5 minutes · Start Here
Before configuring anything, get familiar with how the platform is laid out. The left navigation rail is your primary menu — it runs vertically on the left edge of the screen and holds icon buttons for every major area. Hover over any icon to see its label. On mobile, the rail moves to the bottom of the screen as a tab bar.
📍Find it:Left edge of screen — always visible
1
Log in at job-dox.ai
Use the Owner credentials created during sign-up. You'll land on the Portfolio (project list) view.
2
Click the Advanced Tools icon in the rail
This is the wrench icon near the bottom. A slide-out panel shows links to every specialized tool in the platform. Browse through it — you'll use many of these as you grow.
3
Click through each main section
Try Portfolio, My Day, Messages, and Dispatch. Don't change anything — just get your bearings. This is where your team will spend their days.
⚙️
Step 2 — Fill Out General Settings
Admins Only · ~10 minutes · Most Important Step
General Settings is where you configure everything that identifies your company. This information flows into invoices, documents, client-facing portals, and every AI-generated communication. Get it right once here and it populates everywhere automatically.
📍Find it:Left rail → Settings → General tab
Company Name
Appears on all invoices & documents
Primary Phone
Used for SMS and client contact
Business Address
Drives mapping and office assignment
Industry
Tailors AI responses to your trade
Company Logo
Shows on all client-facing documents
Tax Rate & O&P %
Defaults for all new invoices
⚠️
Don't skip this. If your company name or address is missing, invoices, reports, and the customer portal will look incomplete when sent to clients or adjusters.
Also configure in General Settings:
W
Work Types
The service lines you perform — Water Mitigation, Fire & Smoke, Mold Remediation, etc. Each gets a name and color. Mark ones that need CortexAI workflows built.
S
Statuses
Project lifecycle stages (New Lead, Active, Completed, etc.). Each can have a color and an auto-trigger keyword — when a task with that keyword is completed, the project status advances automatically.
P
Project Types
The loss/job categories that appear in the New Project dropdown — Water Damage, Fire & Smoke, Storm Damage, etc.
B
Budget Categories
Reusable cost categories for tracking — Labor, Materials, Equipment, Subcontractors, etc. Applied to projects in the Finance tab. Color-code each for visual clarity.
L
Pinned Line Items
Pre-configured scope items that appear as quick-add suggestions per work type when building invoices. Set up your most common line items here to save time on every job.
🏢
Step 3 — Set Up Offices
Admins Only · ~5 minutes
Offices represent your physical locations or service territories. Even if you only have one location, you must create at least one office record. Offices control how projects are auto-assigned, how staff are grouped, and where review request links point.
📍Find it:Settings → Offices tab
1
Click "Add Office" and fill in your primary location
Name it something clear (e.g., "Oklahoma City HQ" or "Main Office"). Enter the full address.
2
Click "Geocode Address" after saving
This converts the address to GPS coordinates so the system can automatically assign new projects to the nearest office based on the job site address.
3
Paste your Google Business review URL
This is the link used when sending review requests to clients. Find it in your Google Business Profile dashboard.
4
Repeat for additional locations
Add branches or territories as needed. Staff members are then assigned to their home office in the Staff tab.
👥
Step 4 — Invite Staff & Assign Permissions
Admins Only · ~10 minutes
Add your team and assign each person a permission level. The platform uses an 11-tier system (0–10) to control what each person can see and do. Set this up correctly now to avoid access issues after launch.
📍Find it:Settings → Staff tab
LevelRoleWhat They Can Access
10Admin / OwnerEverything — all pages, all settings, billing, API keys, phone setup
8–9Director / Sr. ManagerAll projects, reports, dispatch, payroll, team management. No billing.
5–7Project ManagerAll projects + Finance, Scope, Shifts, DryDox, ContentsDox, EstimateDox tabs
2–4Field Tech / CrewOverview, Notes, Media, Tasks, Contacts, Messages on assigned projects only
0Vendor / SubcontractorMy Day view with assigned tasks only. No project details or internal data.
💡
Tip: Each staff member needs a "System Role" field set (e.g., Project Manager, Field Technician, Estimator). CortexAI uses this to suggest automatic task assignments when building workflows.
🏗️
Step 5 — Configure Project Types & Work Types
Admins Only · ~8 minutes · Required Before CortexAI
Project Types and Work Types tell the platform what kind of work you do. They power the AI workflows, budget categories, pinned scope items, and portfolio filters. Configure them now — CortexAI cannot generate accurate workflows until they exist.
📍Find it:Settings → General → Work Types section & Project Types section
PT
Project Types = the big loss categories
Water Damage, Fire & Smoke, Mold Remediation, Storm Damage, Biohazard, Reconstruction. These appear in the New Project dropdown and portfolio filters.
WT
Work Types = the service phases within a project
Water Mitigation, Structural Drying, Demo, Reconstruction, Pack-Out. Choose a color for each — these colors appear on project cards and reports.
Why this matters for AI: In Step 7, CortexAI will use your Work Types to generate complete, phase-by-phase task workflows. The more accurately you configure them here, the more useful the AI output will be.
📞
Step 6 — Set Up Phone & Calls
Admins Only · ~8 minutes · Required for SMS & Calls
Cortex includes a complete phone system powered by Twilio. Your dedicated number handles both inbound calls and all SMS messaging across the platform. Without this step, text messages and click-to-call features will not work.
📍Find it:Settings → Phone & Calls tab
1
Company Phone Number
Enter your dedicated Twilio number. This number is used for all inbound and outbound calls, plus every SMS sent from the platform.
2
Call Groups
Create groups of staff who ring simultaneously when a call comes in (e.g., "Business Hours Team"). Set one group as active. Staff must have a phone number on their profile to be included.
3
Recording Disclosure
A message read aloud to callers before they connect — required in many states for call recording compliance.
AI
Call Transcriber (Optional)
When enabled, completed calls are automatically transcribed. The AI extracts the caller's name, phone, address, and damage type — and can automatically create a new project if the caller is not already in the system. Costs 1 Cortex Coin per call.
📋 Requires: A Twilio account with Account SID and Auth Token. Contact your Job-Dox account representative if you need help setting up Twilio.
🤖
Step 7 — Build Workflows in CortexAI (MindFlow)
Owners & Managers · ~10 minutes per Work Type
CortexAI's MindFlow tool generates complete, phase-by-phase task workflows using AI. You describe a job type and it builds the entire workflow — which can then be saved as a template. When a new project is created, that template auto-populates the task list instantly. This is the setup investment that pays off on every single job.
📍Find it:Advanced Tools → CortexAI (opens in new tab) — or Settings → CortexAI → Open
1
Open CortexAI in the same browser session as the portal
Important: both the portal and MindFlow must be open in the same browser. They share data through the browser's memory — no login needed for MindFlow itself.
2
Select a Work Type and describe the job
Type something like: "Create a complete workflow for a residential water damage mitigation job including emergency response, water extraction, structural drying, monitoring, and final documentation."
3
Review and edit the generated workflow
The AI produces a structured, phase-by-phase task list. Read through it and adjust anything that doesn't match how your company actually operates. Add company-specific steps, rename tasks, reorder phases.
4
Save as a template and push to Job-Dox
Save the workflow template tied to the matching Work Type. It syncs back to the portal automatically. Repeat for every Work Type you configured in Step 5.
1 Cortex Coin per workflow generation
📄
Step 8 — Set Up Document Templates
Admins & Managers · ~5 minutes
Document templates are reusable forms — contracts, authorization forms, work orders — that auto-fill with project and client details when applied to a job. Set up your most essential templates now so they're ready for your first project.
📍Find it:Advanced Tools → Document Templates
Start with these three templates — they're used on virtually every job:
1
Work Authorization / Direction to Pay
Client signature authorizing your company to perform work and assigning insurance benefits to you.
2
Scope of Work / Estimate
Line-item scope template used for client and adjuster communication.
3
Certificate of Completion
Signed by the client when the job is finished, confirming satisfactory completion.
💡
Tip: Open a document preview after creating a template. Your company name, logo, and address should auto-populate from General Settings. If they don't, revisit Step 2.
🚀
Step 9 — Create Your First Project
Project Managers & Above · ~5 minutes · Go Live
Everything from Steps 1–8 comes together here. Create a test project — or your first real job — and walk through the full workflow to confirm everything is wired up correctly.
📍Find it:Portfolio → + New Project button (top right)
1
Fill out the new project form
Enter client name, phone, email, property address, project type, and work types. The project number (JD-YYYY-NNN) is auto-generated.
2
Toggle on workflow auto-load
If you built templates in Step 7, a banner shows how many are ready. Toggle on "Load Workflow Templates" and all tasks will be added automatically on project creation.
3
Assign tasks to team members in the Tasks tab
Open the project, go to Tasks, and assign each task or phase to the right staff member. Assigned team members see these tasks in their My Day view.
4
Generate your first document
Go to the Documents tab, select a template, generate it, and confirm the company info auto-populated correctly.
5
Click through every project tab
Overview, Tasks, DryDox, Contacts, Media, Documents, Finance, Shifts, Scope, Messages, Calls, Project Report — know where everything lives before your team starts using it.
✅ You're live. If you can create a project, apply a workflow, assign tasks, and generate a document — your platform is fully operational.
Part 2
Daily Operations

The views and tools your team will use every single day — from checking in on projects to communicating with clients and tracking time.

📋
Project Portfolio
All Staff (visibility varies by permission)
The main landing page showing all active projects as cards or a list. Each card displays project name, client, address, status badge, work type color pills, task count, and (for managers) budget health. Filter by status, search by name or address, sort by various criteria, and toggle between grid and list view. Project cards show a financial health badge (green/yellow/red) visible to users with budget access.
📍Find it:Left rail → Projects (first icon) — default page after login
🔒
Permission gates: Levels 0–2 see only personally assigned projects. Levels 3–4 see their office's projects. Level 5+ sees all projects. Budget data hidden below level 5. "Add Project" button only visible at level 5+.
📅
My Day — Daily Planner & Calendar
All Staff
A personal daily planning page with a mini calendar on the left and a time-slotted schedule on the right. Shows tasks assigned to the user across all projects, personal appointments, and a private checklist. A live "now line" shows the current time on the schedule. Managers (level 5+) can use a dropdown to view another team member's full day — useful for dispatching and scheduling.
📍Find it:Left rail → My Day (calendar icon)
💡
For managers: Use the staff dropdown at the top to check anyone's schedule before assigning new work. Calendar days with tasks show an amber dot indicator.
🗺️
Dispatch Board
Project Managers & Above (level 5+)
A visual scheduling board for dispatching crews and equipment to job sites. Resources (people, vehicles, equipment) run down the left side; time slots run across the top. Drag appointments to reschedule. Includes a Google Maps view with project location pins and driving routes — useful for proximity-based dispatch when managing multiple active jobs. Resources are color-coded and can represent any dispatchable unit. All data syncs in real time across devices.
📍Find it:Left rail → Dispatch
📋 Requires: Office addresses geocoded in Settings → Offices for map proximity features to work.
✅
All Tasks — Company-Wide View
Project Managers & Above (level 5+)
A unified view of every task across every project in the company — without having to click into each project individually. Filter by project, assigned staff, status (open/completed), and priority. Clicking any task navigates directly to that project's Tasks tab. Lower-permission users see only tasks from their assigned projects.
📍Find it:Left rail → All Tasks (checkmark icon)
💬
Message Center
All Staff
A centralized hub for all SMS conversations across the company. Inbound and outbound text messages are organized into threads. Conversations are linked to projects when possible. Inbound texts from unknown numbers create new threads. Send new messages directly from this panel. All messages also appear within the relevant project's Messages tab.
📍Find it:Left rail → Messages (chat icon)
📋 Requires: Twilio phone number configured in Settings → Phone & Calls.
⏱️
Clock In / Clock Out
All Staff
Record billable time against a specific project. Staff select their task or activity, confirm their rate, and clock in. While active, a green pulsing dot appears in the rail. Clocking out automatically records the shift with duration, rate, and calculated labor cost. Only one project can be clocked into at a time — switching auto-closes the previous shift with a note. All shifts feed into the project's Shifts tab and the Payroll module.
📍Find it:Any project → Clock In button — or clock icon in the left rail / mobile bottom nav
Part 3
Inside a Project — Every Tab Explained

Click any project from the Portfolio to open the project detail view. Tabs appear across the top — which ones you see depends on your permission level.

🏠
Overview Tab
All Staff · Default first tab
The project's home base. Shows all core details at a glance: project name, client, address, type, status, auto-generated project number, and assigned staff with their roles and permission levels. Also contains the daily notes log — a chronological journal where team members record what happened each day (with timestamp and author). Work type toggle switches drive CortexAI automations. The Client Portal toggle controls whether the homeowner can view project updates through the customer-facing portal.
📍Find it:Project → Overview tab (shown by default)
💡
Daily Notes: Each note shows the author's name and timestamp. Notes can be flagged "visible to client" — only those notes appear in the customer portal. Use daily notes as your project journal for documentation and adjuster communication.
👤
Contacts Tab
All Staff
Manage everyone associated with a project — homeowners, adjusters, insurance agents, mortgage companies, property managers. Each contact has a name, email, phone, and role (free-text field, e.g., "Adjuster", "Insured", "Mortgage Co."). Contacts are used for sending SMS, emails, review requests, and document signing requests throughout the project.
📍Find it:Project → Contacts tab
📸
Media Tab — Photos & Files
All Staff
Upload, organize, and view photos and files for the project. Organize photos into folders (Before, After, Moisture Readings, Equipment, etc.). Supports drag-and-drop upload, camera capture on mobile, and bulk upload. Every upload records the timestamp and uploader's name automatically. Photo galleries feed into the Project Report tab for comprehensive documentation packages.
📍Find it:Project → Media tab
✅
Tasks Tab
All Staff (vendors see only assigned tasks)
Full task management for the project. Create tasks, assign to staff, set due dates, and mark complete. Each task has a threaded comment system — comments can trigger SMS notifications to the assigned team member. Tasks can be created manually or auto-populated from CortexAI workflow templates on project creation. When a task with a status trigger keyword is completed, the project status automatically advances to the next stage.
📍Find it:Project → Tasks tab
💡
Status auto-advance: In Settings → Statuses, each status stage can have a trigger keyword. When a task containing that keyword is completed, the project moves to the next status automatically — no manual update needed.

Per-project financial tracking. The AI Analysis button sends the project's financials to Claude for a plain-English profitability analysis — identifying cost overruns, budget risks, and optimization opportunities.

1 Cortex Coin per AI financial analysis

Each shift shows staff name, clock-in and clock-out times, total hours, billing rate, labor cost, pay type (Regular/Overtime), and billable status. All Staff (upload/view) · PM+ for signing workflows
Upload, organize, preview, download, and email project documents. Apply document templates to generate pre-filled contracts and forms. Signing status (pending/signed/completed) is tracked in real time.

Part 4
Specialized Restoration Tools

Industry-specific modules that handle documentation and billing unique to restoration work — drying records, contents inventories, estimates, and adjuster communications.

💧
DryDox — Complete Drying Documentation System
Field Techs & PM (level 5+) · Water damage jobs
A complete drying documentation system covering every aspect of the water damage drying process — from initial moisture readings through daily monitoring to final dry standards. Contains six sub-modules organized as tabs within DryDox. Supports multi-floor and multi-room layouts. Mobile-optimized for field use. LiDAR scanning integration available on compatible iOS/Android devices for automatic room measurement.
📍Find it:Project → DryDox tab
FP
Floor Plan Editor
Build interactive room layouts on a canvas. Add rooms with dimensions (manually or via LiDAR scan), place them visually, and mark moisture reading points within each room. Points are numbered and color-coded by reading value (green=dry, yellow=elevated, red=wet). Supports multiple floors and suites.
MR
Moisture & Psychrometric Readings
Log daily moisture readings per point and atmospheric readings (temperature, relative humidity, grains per pound, dew point) at chamber and room level. Color-coded history table tracks progress toward IICRC dry standards. Readings feed into the S500 compliance checker.
EQ
Equipment Inventory & Placement
Track all drying equipment deployed — air movers, dehumidifiers, air scrubbers, heaters. Each piece has type, make/model, serial number, room assignment, start/end dates. Equipment can be placed in rooms on the floor plan. Deployment duration drives billing calculations using your price list rates.
SC
Scope / Billing
Generates billable line items from DryDox data — equipment rental charges, monitoring fees, and other drying costs. Review and adjust before pushing items to the main project Scope tab for invoicing.
RP
Drying Report PDF Generator
Generates a professional PDF drying report with floor plan layouts, moisture reading tables, psychrometric data, equipment maps, S500 compliance status, and a narrative summary. Formatted for insurance submission. Reports are versioned — generating a new report does not overwrite previous ones. Saved automatically to the project's Documents tab.
S5
S500 Compliance Checker
Evaluates the project's drying data against ANSI/IICRC S500 standards — checking psychrometric conditions, equipment-to-area ratios, monitoring frequency, and dry-standard achievement. Shows a compliance dashboard for each requirement.
ESX
ESX Sketch Export (Xactimate)
Exports the DryDox floor plan in ESX format — the native sketch format used by Xactimate. Allows drying documentation to be imported directly into Xactimate estimates without re-drawing rooms.
📦
ContentsDox — Contents Inventory & Schedule of Loss
Contents Specialists, PM (level 5+) · Fire/smoke/water losses
A complete contents documentation system for recording personal property affected by a loss. Each item record includes room location, description, quantity, condition, age, replacement cost (RCV), actual cash value (ACV), and photos. The AI comparable search feature finds current replacement prices from online retailers automatically. Generates a professional Schedule of Loss PDF for insurance submission with all items, values, and totals.
📍Find it:Project → ContentsDox tab
1 Cortex Coin per AI comparable price search
📐
EstimateDox — Good / Better / Best Estimate Builder
Estimators, PM (level 5+)
Create tiered estimates for clients with three pricing levels: Good (economy), Better (standard), and Best (premium). Each tier has its own set of line items and prices. The estimate is presented in a side-by-side comparison format that clients can review and choose from. The accepted tier feeds into the project's financial tracking for budget comparison. Multiple estimate revisions can be saved.
📍Find it:Project → EstimateDox tab
✍️
Adjuster Response Bot ✦ AI
Project Managers, Owners
Generates professional responses to adjuster emails and customer communications. The AI reads the received message along with the project's daily notes, company information, and industry context to craft a detailed rebuttal grounded in IICRC S500/S520 standards. Select the sender's role (adjuster, homeowner, insurance agent), provide custom instructions to guide tone or focus, and generate the response. Edit inline, copy to clipboard, or insert directly into an email draft. Regenerate as many times as needed.
📍Find it:Project → Messages tab → AI icon on any received message
1 Cortex Coin per response generated

📍Find it:Left rail → Payroll — or Advanced Tools → Payroll
📍
MarketDox — SEO Yard Sign Pages
Admins & Owners
An automated local SEO tool that generates public "digital yard sign" web pages for completed projects. When a project status changes to Completed, the system automatically publishes a page at a URL like job-dox.ai/pros/your-company/water-mitigation-tulsa-ok — with Schema.org structured data for Google indexing. Re-opening a project automatically unpublishes its yard sign. A dashboard shows all published signs with locations. Connects to Google Ads and Analytics data via Zapier for ROI tracking.
📍Find it:Advanced Tools → MarketDox
Fully automatic: No action needed beyond completing a project. Yard signs are created and published automatically. A sitemap at /sitemap-yards.xml helps search engines discover all pages.
📋 Optional setup: Connect Google Ads and Analytics via Zapier through the MarketDox setup wizard to track campaign ROI in your dashboard.
Part 6
AI Features — All Cortex Intelligence Tools

Every AI feature is powered by the Anthropic Claude API. All AI features consume Cortex Coins — your AI credit balance tracked in Settings → Cortex Coins.

✦
Cortex Copilot — AI Chat Assistant AI
All Staff
A slide-out AI chat panel trained specifically as a restoration industry project management assistant. Ask questions about your company data in plain language — the AI has access to all project data the current user can see. Ask things like "Which projects are overdue?", "What's our total revenue this month?", "Which jobs need moisture readings today?", or "Who is assigned to the Elm Street project?" The AI responds conversationally with insights, summaries, and suggestions.
📍Find it:Left rail → Cortex Copilot (sparkle icon) — or mobile bottom nav → Cortex
1 Cortex Coin per response
💡
Note: Conversation history is maintained within the session but not saved across page reloads. When coins are exhausted, Copilot will indicate that AI features are paused until the next billing cycle.
🧠
MindFlow — AI Workflow Generator AI
Owners, Managers, Operations Directors
A standalone AI tool for building restoration workflows as interactive mind maps. Select a Work Type, describe the job type and requirements, and the AI generates a complete multi-phase workflow with tasks, role assignments, and dependencies organized visually. Drag and drop to edit nodes, collapse phases, and add custom tasks. Save as a template and push to Job-Dox — it becomes the auto-loaded task list for any new project of that work type. MindFlow reads your staff data and work types directly from the portal session.
📍Find it:Advanced Tools → CortexAI (opens new tab at /mindflow.html)
⚠️
Important: MindFlow must be opened in the same browser session as the portal. Both tools communicate through your browser's memory — no separate login or API key needed.
1 Cortex Coin per workflow generation

Vendor & Subcontractor Manager
Project Managers & Above
Full vendor management with a master-detail layout. Left side lists all vendors with compliance status badges. Estimators & Above
Manage reusable price lists that feed the Scope/Invoice tab, DryDox billing, and EstimateDox. Each price list contains line items with descriptions, units (SF, LF, EA, HR, etc.), and prices. API & Integrations
Admins only (level 8+)
Two-tab panel for external connectivity. API Keys tab: Generate, view, and revoke API keys for the Job-Dox Open API. Each key has a name and configurable scopes (projects:read, projects:write, contacts:read, etc.). Key value is shown once on creation — store it securely. Keys are rate-limited to 60 requests per minute. Apps tab: Browse and connect third-party integrations including webhook and Zapier connections. Outbound webhooks can be registered to trigger on platform events and are auto-disabled after 10 consecutive failures.
📍Find it:Advanced Tools → API & Integrations

Field Tech Mobile App (PWA)
Field Technicians — all permission levels
A lightweight progressive web app designed for technicians working on job sites. Provides quick access to clock in/out, photo capture, daily note entry, and task viewing — without the full complexity of the main portal. Optimized for one-hand mobile use. Customer Portal — Homeowner View
Homeowners / Clients (external users)
A public-facing portal where clients can view the status of their restoration project. Authenticated by matching the viewer's email to a project contact. Shows project status, client-visible daily notes (only notes with the "visible to client" flag enabled), project photos, and document signing status. Does not show internal notes, financial data, or any staff-only information.
📍Find it:Enable per-project via: Overview tab → Client Portal toggle — then share the link with your client
